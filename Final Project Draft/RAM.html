A Horrible Mistake

As many probably know, all of this resulted in the Indian Removal Act of 1830, where around 60,000 Native Americans were forcibly relocated to west of the Mississippi River. Over 4,000 died from multiple factors such as starvation and exposure. The Native Americans and their descendants are still treated poorly by the United States, and it makes one wonder what could have been done instead. Perhaps, if George Washington and his government was more firm in trying to respect Native American traditions rather than trying to “civilize” them, there could have been more done to help them. Regardless, it remains in history as a series of horrible tragedies, and should be remembered as such.
